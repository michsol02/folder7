RESPONSIBLE 100 BENCHMARKS

Since 2012, Responsible 100 has helped hundreds of businesses to discover the challenges and opportunities that a wide range of responsibility issues present. Issue by issue, no matter how high a priority for a given business, we've understood and described different standards of business response as either POOR, OKAY, GOOD or EXCELLENT. 

Further, we've collaborated with hundreds of supporter organsanisations including think tanks, NGOs, campaign groups, unions, trade bodies, government and academia. Together, we socialise the problems that each issue presents and we work collaboratively to explore and create possible solutions.

As such there is an incredible diveristy of opinion, knowlegde and experience brought to bear in the exploration and appreciation of all the issues we examine.

Issue by issue, the further and deeper we've explored, the more we've discovered.

Every example of a new business approach to a given responsibility issue has been recorded and attributed to one of the four performance standard buckets of POOR, OKAY, GOOD and EXCELLENT.

So, every time a business assesses against an issue and the associated Responsible 100 scorecard benchmark, our collective, shared knowledge and understanding is broadened.

We've created viruous circles around these critical social, environmental and ethical issues. As businesses discover and learn more, and consider their current policies and practices in a new light, and describe what they do now and which opportunities to change and improve they intend to pursue, the whole Responsible 100 community benefits. With every use, our benchmarks are improved for the next participant. 

"Responsible 100 has perfected the art of bringing companies and NGOs together to explore and share challenges, learning and practical know how, in a safe space. It's for people who are passionate about making a difference. Not for those who want to talk through the theory of how to make a difference, but those keen to learn about what works in today's context to ready their organisations for the future."
Head of Corporate Responsibility & Employee Engagement, Virgin Trains
